Research Overview

My doctoral research in Professor Merav Opher's group at Boston University and the SHIELD (Solar-wind with Hydrogen Ion Exchange and Large-scale Dynamics) Drive Center focuses on plasma instabilities and turbulence in the heliosheath. I contributed to developing a three-dimensional MHD model with block-adaptive mesh refinement on HPC clusters, collaborating with the University of Michigan. I revealed that charge exchange forces drive Rayleigh-Taylor instability while velocity shear triggers Kelvin-Helmholtz instability and demonstrated their periodic evolution from coupled growth to decoupling phases. I further collaborated with University of Alabama to develop power spectra and kurtosis workflows to track energy cascade from large-scale instabilities to fully developed turbulence.

During my master's and undergraduate research at Peking University, I conducted statistical analysis of multi-satellite data investigating magnetospheric responses to solar wind variations, developing automated event detection algorithms and applying Superposed Epoch Analysis. This resulted in two first-author publications in Journal of Geophysical Research: Space Physics and provided practical experience with large-scale observational datasets applicable to solar remote-sensing data.

Current Research Projects

Instabilities along the Axis of the Heliospheric Jets

Completed

This project focuses on discovering and characterizing plasma instabilities in the heliosheath, the region between the termination shock and the heliopause, providing new insights into how instabilities influence global heliospheric dynamics.

Key Contributions

  • Developed a two-stage modeling strategy that first obtains a coarse-grid steady state and then switches to fine-grid time-domain simulations, enabling precise identification of instability onset conditions in the heliosheath.
  • Revealed for the first time that magnetic tension and charge exchange act together to decelerate the solar wind, creating a distinct and stable low-speed region that provides the necessary conditions for RT/KH instability growth.
  • Extended the focus from the heliopause into the heliosheath interior, showing that velocity shear formed within the low-speed region triggers Kelvinâ€“Helmholtz instability inside the heliosheath.
Ma et al. 2024 (ApJ)

Instability-Driven Turbulence in the Heliosheath

Ongoing

This project investigates how large-scale heliosheath instabilities generate small-scale turbulence. By quantifying energy cascade and intermittency evolution, I established the framework linking these instabilities to developed turbulence in the heliosheath.

Key Contributions

  • Built automated workflows to compute magnetic-field power spectra and kurtosis from MHD simulations, enabling quantitative tracking of energy cascade and intermittency.
  • Established the physical picture from large-scale RT and KH instabilities to small-scale turbulence within the inner region of the heliosphere, reflecting the complex coupling between the magnetic field structure and plasma dynamics, providing insight essential for predicting heliospheric ENA signatures and cosmic-ray modulation.
Ma et al. 2026 (in preparation)

Past Projects

Research on Development of Rayleigh-Taylor-Like Driven Instability in Heliosheath with a Multi-Ion Treatment

Completed

This study applied a multi-ion (MI) MHD model, treating thermal solar wind ions and pickup ions (PUIs) as separate fluids, to investigate the impact of PUIs on Rayleighâ€“Taylor instability. By calculating growth rates in the MI model and comparing them with single-ion (SI) model results, it revealed differences in instability behavior and quantified the role of PUIs in modifying RT instability.

The Intense Substorm Incidence in Response to Interplanetary Shock Impacts and Influence on Energetic Electron Fluxes at Geosynchronous Orbit

Completed

This study examined how interplanetary shocks trigger magnetospheric substorms and alter high-energy electron flux in geosynchronous orbits. Using 20+ years of data, it quantified shock impacts on substorm intensity and electron response, revealing the causal chain from shocks to substorms to electron dynamics. The results provide key insights for space weather forecasting and satellite protection.

Energetic Electron Enhancement and Dropout Echoes Induced by Solar Wind Dynamic Pressure Decrease: The Effect of Phase Space Density Profile

Completed

This study revealed the mechanism behind high-energy electron enhancement and loss triggered by decreases in solar wind dynamic pressure. It highlighted the key role of phase space density distribution and showed that its peak location across L-shells determines the direction of radial diffusion, producing electron enhancement or loss. These findings provide new insights into radiation belt electron dynamics.

Forecasting Warm Plasma Cloak Particle Dynamics in the Inner Magnetosphere Using Machine Learning

Completed

This study developed neural network models to forecast plasma distributions from satellite time-series data. By creating a 2D spatial model of warm plasma cloak particle distributions and visualizing their temporal evolution, it provided deeper insight into magnetospheric dynamics.
